Margin

Margin Collapse

Margins are the outermost element of a box. Positive margin values are intuitive, however, negative margins, and margin collapse are trickier and need explanation.

BOX2

BOX1 has a bottom margin of 10px and BOX2 has a top margin of 20px. Therefore, we would expect them to be 30 pixels apart. However, the bottom margin of BOX1 collapses with the top margin of BOX2 and they end up 20 pixels apart.

BOX4

Now, the top margins of .box3 and .box4 collapse and the space between BOX4 and the paragraph above it is only 30px instead of the expected 40px. Margins of parent and child elements collapse only when the parent does not have border, padding, and content.

Negative margins

Negative margins pull the adjacent elements closer.

Note

Margin collapse and negative margins are shown here in the vertical direction ( top and bottom ). It is worth noting that collapse and negative margins behave similarly in the horizontal direction ( left and right ).
